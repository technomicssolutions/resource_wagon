How to prepare your Resume

The resume often builds the first impression of the candidate. It's the opportunity that you get to gain the prospective employers' interest.

Below are a few resume writing tips.

  • Keep the resume well organized and brief. No employer would like to spend more than 3-4 minutes going through a profile.
  • Provide your personal details like full name, DOB, Nationality, Contact Information etc.There's no requirement to include your marital status, relegion etc.
  • List your educational as well as your professional qualifications and grades in chronological order.
  • List your IT/Software Skills.
  • 'Last things first'-That's the thumb rule for providing your employment history.
  • Mention your proficiency in languages.
  • Today's corporate culture demands well rounded individuals. So don't forget to mention your personal interests.
  • Include references if possible.
  • Above all, eliminate typos, spelling mistakes, grammatical errors and try to keep the language simple.

How to prepare for your interview:

If resume builds your first impression, interview cements it. Devote time to prepare properly for the interview.

Below are a few interview preparation tips.

  • Dress in smart business attire for the interview.
  • Gather information about the Company, its profile and the role you are going to take up.
  • Keep relevant personal documentations such as resumes, qualification certificates etc handy.
  • Instead of asking for an exaggerated salary package explain why you are worth more.
  • Arrive a little early for the interview so that you have time to compose yourself.
  • Greet the interviewer with a smile and a firm handshake, making eye contact.
  • During the interview, you will be assessed on your strengths and weaknesses. Try to be clear and honest.
  • Prepare some relevant questions to ask the employer about the role you are going to take up.
  • Try not to initiate discussions on remuneration at the first interview, but be open if asked.
  • Avoid making negative remarks about your current employer.
  • Leave the interviewer with a good impression.
  • Above all, conduct yourself as if you are determined to get the job you are discussing.
